01 · The problem

You can't underwrite what you can't observe.

Institutional capital is priced on evidence. In most African markets the evidence exists — thousands of listings published every week — but it is scattered across portals, languages and currencies, with no one turning it into a benchmark you can cite.

02 · The engine

Collected weekly. Cleaned once. Cited forever.

STEP 1 — GATHER

Continuous market observation

Automated gathering of publicly listed market evidence in each market, on a weekly cadence.

STEP 2 — CLEAN

Deduplicate & standardise

One schema across languages and currencies; duplicates removed; outliers winsorized before any median is published.

STEP 3 — GUARD

Minimum-sample rules

No district figure is shown unless enough observations stand behind it — every number states its basis.

STEP 4 — PUBLISH

Benchmarks & reports

District medians, market landscapes and monthly PDF reports, refreshed automatically.

Asking prices are never presented as transaction prices. Medians are preferred over means. Research figures are quoted verbatim from their publishers — never silently blended. Where the data is insufficient, the terminal shows "—", not an estimate.
