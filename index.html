How We Build Our Data

Methodology & Data Standards

Data Sources

Ironwood Intelligence aggregates data from multiple complementary source types to maximise coverage and cross-reference accuracy.

  • Public listing portals — scraped at regular intervals per market
  • Developer direct sources — price lists, launches, and absorption data
  • Agent network — verified asking and achieved rents from in-market agents
  • Transactional registries — where available and accessible by market
  • Field research — direct market surveys conducted by our team

Collection Process

Data is collected via automated scraping pipelines operating at defined intervals per market, supplemented by manual field collection. Each record is timestamped and source-tagged at the point of collection.

Collection cadence varies by market. Live markets (e.g. Morocco) are updated monthly. Expanding and pilot markets are updated quarterly or when new data becomes available.

Verification & Cleaning

Raw collected data goes through a multi-stage cleaning pipeline before entering the platform database.

  • Price outlier removal using interquartile range trimming (top and bottom 2% excluded)
  • Null and incomplete records flagged and excluded from aggregations
  • Price-per-square-metre normalisation across different listing formats
  • Currency and unit standardisation per market
  • Asset class classification using structured taxonomies
  • Geographic tagging and district-level geocoding

Deduplication

Duplicate records across collection runs are identified and removed using a composite matching approach. Records are considered duplicates when all of the following fields match: city, submarket, title, price, and size (m²).

Where duplicates are found, the earliest collected record is retained and all subsequent identical records are excluded from aggregations. Listings that remain active across multiple collection periods are tracked as continuous observations, not new entries.

Metric Definitions

  • Median price/m² — the middle value of all cleaned, deduplicated price-per-square-metre observations for a given market segment. Preferred over mean due to robustness against outliers.
  • Listing volume — count of unique, deduplicated active listings within a market segment at time of collection.
  • Rent benchmark — median DH/m²/month (or local currency equivalent) for active rental listings in a defined submarket and asset class.
  • Sale benchmark — median DH/m² (or local currency equivalent) for active sale listings.

Observed vs. Estimated

Ironwood Intelligence distinguishes clearly between observed and estimated data points. All benchmarks published on the platform are based on observed listing or transactional evidence unless otherwise stated.

Where data coverage is insufficient for reliable aggregation (fewer than 5 observations in a given segment), metrics are suppressed and marked as unavailable rather than estimated.

Limitations & Disclaimers

  • Listing data reflects asking prices, not necessarily achieved transaction prices
  • Coverage depth varies significantly by market and asset class
  • Data should be used as one input among several in investment and underwriting decisions
  • Ironwood Intelligence does not provide valuation opinions or investment advice
  • Historical comparability may be affected by changes in collection methodology

About Ironwood Intelligence

Built to bring rigour
to African real estate data

Ironwood Intelligence is a real estate data and market intelligence platform focused exclusively on African markets. We provide institutional-grade data infrastructure for markets that have historically lacked it.

Our Mission

Transparency in opaque markets

Africa is home to the world's fastest-growing cities and youngest population. Yet real estate data across the continent remains fragmented, unreliable, and inaccessible to the decision-makers who need it most.

Ironwood Intelligence was built to fix that. We aggregate, clean, and structure property data from across the continent so investors, developers, lenders, and institutions can make decisions grounded in evidence rather than assumption.

Based in Meydan Free Zone, Dubai, UAE — with a research and data presence across Africa.

What We Build

A data terminal for African real estate

We are building the institutional data layer that African real estate has been missing — comparable to what CoStar, MSCI Real Assets, and RCA provide in more mature markets.

That means not just collecting data, but verifying it, structuring it, and delivering it in formats that fit the workflows of serious institutional buyers: financial models, BI platforms, research reports, and API integrations.
